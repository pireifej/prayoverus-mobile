It sounds like you’ve already built a fantastic, feature-rich foundation. Having the "One-Tap Amen," a testimonial/praise loop, and a tiered title system means you've successfully mapped out the core mechanics of a great community app.
If you already have titles (like "Prayer Warrior"), adding **badges** is a natural next step. While titles show a user's *overall level*, badges are great because they celebrate *specific behaviors* and milestones. They give users unique "mini-quests" to achieve.
Here is how you can design a badge system specifically tailored to drive user-to-user engagement, alongside a couple of other "missing link" ideas since your core tech is already so strong.
## 1. High-Engagement Badge Ideas
To get people praying for others, design badges that reward consistency, variety, and depth of interaction.
### The Consistency Badges
People build habits when they are chasing a streak.
 * **"Midnight Intercessor":** Awarded for tapping "Amen" on a request between 11 PM and 4 AM (great for catching those late-night, heavy-hearted requests).
 * **"Daily Bread":** Awarded for interacting with at least one prayer request 7 days in a row.
### The Community Badges
These encourage users to dig deeper into the app rather than just scrolling the main feed.
 * **"First Responder":** Awarded for being one of the first 3 people to tap "Amen" on a brand-new prayer request. (This ensures no request sits at zero).
 * **"Deep Waters":** Awarded for leaving an encouraging text comment (if your app allows it) on a request, rather than just using the one-tap button.
 * **"The Rejoicer":** Awarded for interacting with or commenting on 5 different Praise Reports/Testimonials.
### The Variety Badges
These push users to pray outside of their usual comfort zone or specific categories.
 * **"Global Heart":** Awarded for praying for requests across 5 different categories (e.g., Health, Finance, Relationships).
## 2. If You Have All the Features, Look at "UX Prominence"
If you have the features but engagement is still lower than you want, the issue might not be *what* features you have, but *when* and *how* users see them.
 * **The Notification "Hook":** When you send an email or push notification about a new request, what does it say? If it just says *"John Doe posted a prayer request,"* it’s easy to swipe away. If it says, *"John Doe is asking for prayer regarding his upcoming surgery tomorrow. Can you lift him up?"* it creates immediate empathy.
 * **The Empty Feed Solution:** When a user opens the app, what is the very first thing they see? If it's a generic dashboard, they might get distracted. Try landing them directly on a "Needs Prayer Right Now" screen with a single, prominent card and a giant, satisfying **"Amen"** button.
## 3. The "Prayer Match" Event (An Alternative to Features)
Sometimes the best feature is a community event. Since your tech stack supports notifications and tracking, you could run a **"Prayer Partner Week"** or a **"24-Hour Prayer Chain."**
 * The app automatically pairs a user up with one other person's request for the week.
 * Their sole "mission" is to check in on that specific person and lift them up. It removes choice paralysis entirely.
> **A Note on Badge Design:** Make sure the badges feel meaningful and respectful of the app's tone. Instead of flashing arcade graphics, use elegant, minimalist, or faith-inspired iconography (like a candle, an open hand, a shield, or a rising sun) that aligns with the solemn and supportive nature of prayer.
